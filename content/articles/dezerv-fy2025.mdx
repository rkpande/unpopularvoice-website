## The Number That Provides Context

In FY2023, Dezerv Investments (the parent entity alone) posted ₹52.58 Cr in revenue against ₹58.40 Cr in total costs. The loss was ₹4.71 Cr.

That is not a rounding error. The model was working — nearly covering its costs on ₹52 Cr in fee and commission income in the company's second full year of operations.

FY2024 reversed it. FY2025 widened it further. The ₹112 Cr consolidated loss in FY2025 is the result of a deliberate scale-up — ₹111 Cr on people, ₹30 Cr on marketing — while revenue has not yet grown proportionally to absorb that cost. Whether that investment is justified depends on AUM trajectory. MCA filings do not disclose AUM.

## Three Entities: What Each Does

**Dezerv Investments Private Limited** — the parent. Holds SEBI Investment Adviser and PMS registrations. Investment team, technology, and client relationships sit here. **Fact:** standalone FY2025 revenue ₹34.60 Cr; standalone loss ₹111.90 Cr.

**Dezerv Distribution Services Private Limited** — SEBI-registered mutual fund distributor. Earns trail commissions on AUM in regular mutual fund plans. Revenue compounds with AUM as long as clients remain invested. **Estimated:** contributes a portion of the ~₹31 Cr incremental consolidated revenue.

**Dezerv Securities Private Limited** — SEBI-registered stockbroker. Executes trades for PMS and discretionary clients. Revenue tied to portfolio turnover. **Estimated:** contributes the remainder of the ~₹31 Cr incremental consolidated revenue.

**On subsidiary economics:** Consolidated PAT (−₹111.98 Cr) minus standalone PAT (−₹111.90 Cr) implies a combined subsidiary loss of approximately ₹0.08 Cr against ~₹31 Cr in incremental revenue. This suggests the subsidiaries are operating near breakeven. **Caveat:** intercompany charges, management fee allocations, and consolidation eliminations may distort this derived figure. The precise subsidiary P&Ls are not separately extracted in available filings.

## FY2024: The Revenue Collapse

**Fact:** standalone revenue fell from ₹52.58 Cr (FY2023) to ₹20.06 Cr (FY2024) — a ₹32.52 Cr decline in a single year. The annual filing gives no explanation.

**Likely explanation 1 — regulatory:** SEBI's 2023 changes to mutual fund distributor expense ratios reduced trail commissions industry-wide. If a meaningful portion of Dezerv's FY2023 revenue came from distribution commissions sitting in the parent entity, those would have been directly affected.

**Likely explanation 2 — structural:** Shifting client mandates from distribution to PMS creates a revenue gap during transition. Distribution generates recurring commissions on existing AUM. PMS management fees accumulate on a new base and take time to ramp.

**Likely explanation 3 — subsidiary ring-fencing:** If FY2024 was the year distribution revenue moved into Dezerv Distribution Services, the parent's standalone line would show the decline while consolidated would be more stable. The FY2024 consolidated figure of ~₹26 Cr vs. standalone ₹20.06 Cr suggests the subsidiaries were already active.

**Unknown from filings:** The actual reason is not stated anywhere in the available documents. The above are inferences only.

## The ₹111 Cr Salary Line

**Fact:** consolidated employee costs were ₹111 Cr in FY2025 — 76% up from ₹63 Cr in FY2024, and 1.69x total consolidated revenue.

**Fact:** standalone parent employee costs were ₹92.60 Cr. **Estimated:** subsidiaries account for ~₹18.4 Cr of the balance.

**Likely explanation:** Wealth management is people-before-revenue. Portfolio managers and relationship managers must be hired before the AUM they will eventually manage arrives. The cost is incurred at hire; the fee income follows — if AUM grows, if clients stay.

**Counterargument:** A 76% YoY increase in consolidated employee costs does not necessarily mean 76% headcount growth. A portion may reflect ESOP charges (accounting expense, not cash), variable pay accruals, or compensation resets for existing employees. The filing does not break down the employee cost line.

**Unknown from filings:** The split between new hires, compensation changes, and ESOP charges is not available. AUM data — the metric that would allow evaluation of whether this spend is generating returns — is not a required MCA disclosure.
