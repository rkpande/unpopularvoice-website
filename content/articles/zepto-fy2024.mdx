## Context: What everyone is saying

Zepto raised $665M in 2024 and its PR machine has been loud: "fastest growing quick commerce platform," "path to profitability," "category leadership." The mainstream coverage celebrated the fundraise and the GMV numbers.

We looked at the MCA filings instead.

## What Doesn't Add Up

<InsightBox type="flag" number="01" title="Marketing spend is outpacing order growth">
  Zepto spent Rs 780 Cr on customer acquisition in FY24 — a 68% jump YoY. Orders grew 44%. When you spend more to acquire customers but get fewer orders per rupee, your CAC efficiency is deteriorating. This is the opposite of what improving unit economics looks like.
</InsightBox>

<InsightBox type="flag" number="02" title="Inventory financing costs buried in 'other expenses'">
  Rs 340 Cr in financing charges appear under operational costs, not finance costs. This is an aggressive accounting choice that flatters the gross margin line. Adjusted gross margin drops from 18% to approximately 11% — still positive, but far less impressive.
</InsightBox>

<InsightBox type="flag" number="03" title="The 'profitability' claim is contribution margin, not EBITDA">
  Zepto announced "contribution margin positive" in Q3 FY24. True — but contribution margin excludes G&A, tech infrastructure, and marketing overhead. EBITDA loss was still Rs 312 Cr that same quarter. The language is technically correct and practically misleading.
</InsightBox>

## Glossary

**CM1 (Contribution Margin 1):** Revenue minus cost of goods sold. Does not include delivery or marketing.

**CM2 (Contribution Margin 2):** CM1 minus variable delivery cost. Zepto turned CM2 positive in FY24.

**CM3 (Contribution Margin 3):** CM2 minus customer acquisition cost per order. The truest measure of unit economics. Still negative for Zepto.

**CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost):** Total marketing and promotion spend divided by new customers acquired in the period.

**EBITDA:** Earnings Before Interest, Tax, Depreciation and Amortisation. The standard measure of operational profitability.

**Burn Rate:** Cash consumed per month. At Zepto's FY24 loss run-rate, approximately Rs 104 Cr per month.
